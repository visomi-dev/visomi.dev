# Product Definition (Your Identity as a System)

To sell yourself as a freelance or position yourself for senior roles, you must stop seeing yourself as an "employee" and start seeing yourself as a "B2B Product" (Business to Business).

Companies don't hire programmers just because; they "buy" solutions to their engineering problems, technical debt, or development bottlenecks.

## 1. The Problem You Solve (The Pain Point)
Companies look for senior profiles because they have complex projects, disorganized teams, or need someone who doesn't just write code, but proposes architecture and scalable systems.
Additionally, the market highly values engineers who know how to use **AI as a multiplier** to deliver software quickly, cleanly, and documented.

## 2. Your Value Proposition
Based on your constraints (`01_design_constrain_block.md`), your main value proposition is:
**"Mature, systems-oriented frontend engineering, scalable architecture, and AI-augmented, delivered with clarity and precision."**

Your virtues as a product:
* **Structural:** You think in components, flows, and architecture before typing.
* **Calm Confidence:** You don't need hype; your code, your systems, and your decisions speak for you.
* **Multiplier:** You use agents and AI tools to do the work of 3 mid-level developers, ensuring senior quality.

## 3. Your Audience (Your "Users")
We must design the site thinking about who is going to use it:

1. **Tech Leads / Engineering Managers:** (Job interviews). They look for code quality, architecture, knowing that you know what you do and how you explain it. They care about seeing clean repositories or deep case studies.
2. **Recruiters / ATS:** (Initial screens). They scan the site in 10 seconds looking for keywords and experience. ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) and AI parsers read your CV's DOM.
3. **Founders / Freelance Clients:** (Business). They care less about specific technology and more about "can you solve my problem on time and without it breaking?". They look for success cases and a portfolio styled like a service catalog.

## 4. Information Architecture (Your Product's "Sitemap")

Your personal "system" should guide these users towards conversion (Hiring you).

* **`/` (Landing Page / Hero):** The "Hook". A minimalist declaration of principles. (e.g., "Software Engineer. System Thinker. AI-Augmented.").
* **`/projects` (Case Studies / Freelance Portfolio):** Structured like Upwork. Clear cards showcasing the problem, the technology used, and the quantifiable result. (For Founders and Tech Leads).
* **`/resume` (Optimized CV):** Strong typography, clean hierarchy, semantic HTML so AIs and ATS can read it perfectly without breaking. (For Recruiters).
* **`/contact`:** The final "Call to Action". A clean, frictionless form.

## 5. Visual Concept (Design Identity)
Your "Vibe":
* **Strict Minimalism:** If a feature doesn't serve a purpose, it's removed.
* **Typography as Structure:** Letters build the space, not boxes.
* **Deep Space:** Use of CSS and the animated background you already have to give that feeling of "structure within vastness" (Your influence from the universe and sci-fi/medieval RPGs).

This definition will guide *every* technical decision we make in Angular. There will not be a `padding` or a `<div>` that is not aligned with the idea that you are a "mature system".
